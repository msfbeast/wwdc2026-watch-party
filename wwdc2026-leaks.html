The Main Event

Siri 2.0 — A Complete Rebuild

Apple's flagship AI overhaul. Powered by Gemini. Built as a standalone app. Designed to replace your relationship with apps themselves.

🧠

Gemini-Powered Core

Apple's partnership with Google for Gemini integration, reportedly valued at ~$1B/year. Not a replacement of Apple's own models — a hybrid approach combining Google's frontier capabilities with Apple's on-device intelligence.

💬

Dedicated Siri App

A full chatbot-style interface — think ChatGPT, but native. Syncs across devices via iCloud. Conversation history auto-deletes for privacy. Persistent across sessions but never stored long-term.

📱

Dynamic Island Integration

Dark, glowing UI anchored in the Dynamic Island. Swipe down from top-center reveals a "Search or Ask" prompt. Siri becomes a persistent overlay, not a dismissed assistant.

🎯

Trigger Methods

  • Wake word: "Hey Siri" (still supported)
  • Power button long-press
  • Swipe down from top-center of screen
  • Dynamic Island tap (contextual)
⚡

New Capabilities

  • Multi-command chaining in a single request
  • On-screen context awareness (reads what you see)
  • Personal data cross-reference across apps
  • Deep App Intents integration for third-party actions
  • Document and photo uploads for analysis
  • Follow-up questions without re-triggering
🔍

In-House Web Search

Apple building its own web search engine as a Perplexity competitor. Siri will surface web results natively without defaulting to Google. A seismic shift in Apple's search strategy — and potentially Google's biggest risk.

🔒

Privacy Architecture

On-device processing for personal queries. Sensitive requests routed through Private Cloud Compute. No conversation data used for training. Auto-deletion policy enforced server-side.

⚙️

Release Status

Internally labeled "beta" or "preview" — Apple managing expectations. Possible waitlist for early access. Phased rollout beginning with developer betas, widening through fall.

⚔️

OpenAI Tension

ChatGPT integration buried in the new Siri. Reports of potential breach-of-contract claim from OpenAI. Apple pivoting away from being seen as a distribution channel for OpenAI's products.

🔀

Third-Party Model Picker

Choose your brain: a dropdown lets users select between Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT as Siri's backing model. An unprecedented move — Apple turning Siri into an AI model aggregator.

What This Means

Siri 2.0 isn't an incremental update. It's Apple's full-throated entry into the AI assistant wars. By offering a model picker, Apple positions Siri as a neutral platform — while betting that most users will stay on the default (Gemini-powered) experience. The dedicated app and Dynamic Island integration suggest Siri becomes the primary interface for many iPhone interactions.

Desktop Platform

macOS 27 — "Big Bear"

The end of the Intel era. Apple Silicon only. The Mac goes touch-first — and gets a big cat name back.

🚫

Intel Mac Support Dropped

MAJOR CHANGE

macOS 27 ends Intel Mac compatibility. Apple Silicon only — M1 and later. The transition that began in 2020 is officially complete. Millions of Intel Macs lose support overnight.

🔄

Rosetta 2 Phase-Out

Full Rosetta 2 support continues through macOS 27, then becomes limited in subsequent releases. The Rosetta 2 era is winding down. Native Apple Silicon is now the only first-class citizen.

💻

Supported Hardware

  • All M-series Macs (M1, M1 Pro/Max/Ultra, M2, M3, M4)
  • MacBook Neo (A18 Pro chip) — the new entry-level Mac
  • iMac, MacBook Air, MacBook Pro, Mac Mini, Mac Studio, Mac Pro (M2 Ultra+)
🐻

Naming: "Big Bear"

Hashed flag filename in codebase references "Big Bear" — Apple returning to California landmark names. The "Big Bear" reference points to Big Bear Lake in the San Bernardino Mountains.

📱

Touch-Optimized UI

Reports of a more touch-friendly interface layer. Larger hit targets, iOS-inspired gestures, and a UI that bridges the gap between Mac and iPad — fueling touchscreen Mac rumors.

🧠

CoreAI Framework

New developer framework for integrating on-device AI into Mac apps. Brings the same Apple Intelligence capabilities from iOS to the desktop, with higher compute budgets thanks to Mac-class hardware.

The Big Picture

macOS 27 is Apple drawing a hard line: the future is Apple Silicon. Dropping Intel now, phasing out Rosetta next. Combined with touch-optimized UI and CoreAI, this is Apple reshaping the Mac for the AI era — and cutting the cord to its x86 past.

Live Blog Priorities

What to Watch

The 10 things that will define whether WWDC 2026 is a triumph or a transition. Ranking the keynote moments by impact.

  1. Siri demo quality — The single most important moment. If Siri 2.0 feels like magic, Apple wins the narrative. If it's clunky, the "beta" label becomes a crutch.
  2. "Beta" framing — How hard does Apple hedge? A "preview" label manages expectations but deflates excitement. The framing matters as much as the features.
  3. Third-party model picker depth — Is the Claude/Gemini/ChatGPT dropdown a real feature or a developer settings panel? Depth here determines if Apple is a platform or a gatekeeper.
  4. In-house search demo — If Apple demos its own web search and it's good, Google's search deal is in serious jeopardy. This is the sleeper story of WWDC.
  5. macOS 27 name — Will they actually call it "Big Bear"? The naming convention signals Apple's priorities and personality. A fun name = confidence. A safe name = caution.
  6. Foldable iPhone APIs — The depth and maturity of foldable APIs will reveal how close the iPhone Fold really is. Are these production-ready or conceptual?
  7. CoreAI / dev APIs — The developer story. CoreAI framework details will determine if the AI platform is real or just Apple Intelligence with a new name.
  8. Hardware teases — Will Apple hint at iPhone Fold, MacBook Pro OLED, or HomePad? Even a "one more thing" moment would send the market into overdrive.
  9. Craig Federighi running keynote — Is this Tim Cook's last WWDC? Does Craig take the lead? The leadership signal matters for Apple's next decade.
  10. Privacy narrative — How does Apple differentiate its AI from OpenAI/Google/Anthropic on privacy? The Private Cloud Compute story needs to be bulletproof.
